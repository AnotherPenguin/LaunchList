101955 Benu Discovered in 1999, Bennu is one of Earth’s closest companions and easiest asteroids to get to in the solar system. However because its orbit crosses the earth’s path it is also a potential threat. Phobos Phobos used to be an asteroid, but was captured by Mars and is now a moon. It has long inspired science fiction writers with the promise of vast quantities of ice under its dusty surface. 624 Hektor This asteroid orbits the L4 Sun-Jupiter lagrange point, at an average distance of 781,000,000 km from the sun. It is the largest of the trojan asteroids, and is also one of the most elongated bodies in the solar system, with a minor diameter of 200 km and a major diameter of 370 km. It even has a small ‘moon’ of its own. Not much else is known about this mysterious body, although its distance from the sun makes it a likely candidate for finding water ice. 7.7 10
